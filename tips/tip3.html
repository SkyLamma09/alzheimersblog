Nutrition & Meal Time Strategies

Maintaining proper nutrition can become challenging as Alzheimer's progresses. These approaches can help ensure adequate food intake while reducing stress:

1. Simplify meals: Offer one or two foods at a time to avoid overwhelming choices. Use plain white plates to make food more visible. Finger foods often work better than utensils in later stages. Cut food into bite-sized pieces to make eating easier.

2. Establish routines: Serve meals at consistent times in a familiar setting. Play soft music to create a calming atmosphere. Use verbal cues like "Here's your delicious soup" to stimulate appetite. Be patient - meals may take much longer than they used to.

3. Address common challenges: For those who forget to eat, offer reminders and supervised meals. For overeating or eating non-food items, lock cabinets and the fridge if needed. For swallowing difficulties, consult a speech therapist about texture modifications.
