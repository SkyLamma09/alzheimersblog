Home Safety Modifications

Creating a safe environment is crucial as Alzheimer's progresses. These modifications can help prevent accidents while maintaining independence:

1. Reduce fall risks: Install grab bars in bathrooms, remove loose rugs, and ensure adequate lighting throughout the home. Use contrasting colors to mark step edges and changes in floor elevation. Consider a monitoring system for nighttime wandering.

2. Secure hazardous items: Lock away cleaning supplies, medications, and sharp objects. Install safety knobs on stoves and consider automatic shut-off devices. Lower the temperature on water heaters to prevent scalding.

3. Simplify the environment: Reduce clutter that can cause confusion or agitation. Create clear pathways between frequently used areas. Label doors and drawers with pictures or words to help with navigation. Keep frequently used items in consistent, visible locations.
