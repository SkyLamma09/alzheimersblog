Communication Strategies

Effective communication becomes increasingly challenging as Alzheimer's progresses. These evidence-based strategies can help maintain connection with your loved one.

Verbal Techniques

  • Speak slowly and clearly using short, simple sentences
  • Ask one question at a time and allow plenty of time for responses
  • Use names instead of pronouns ("Mary, your coat" vs. "Here's your coat")
  • Avoid correcting inaccurate statements unless absolutely necessary

Non-Verbal Approaches

  • Maintain eye contact at their eye level
  • Use gentle touch to convey connection
  • Demonstrate actions rather than just explaining
  • Pay attention to tone - your voice may communicate more than words

When Communication Breaks Down

If your loved one becomes agitated:

  1. Stay calm and speak softly
  2. Validate their feelings ("I see this is upsetting you")
  3. Redirect to a different activity or topic
  4. Take a break if needed - frustration is contagious
