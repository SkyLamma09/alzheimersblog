A Daughter's Reflection

Watching my father fade into the shadows of Alzheimer’s was like losing him piece by piece. The strong man who once guided me through life became dependent, often scared, and sometimes unrecognizable. Still, I held on—learning to meet him in his world instead of trying to drag him back into mine. It was one of the hardest, most heartbreaking things I’ve ever done, yet also the most deeply meaningful.

I kept a journal throughout the years, capturing small moments: the warmth of his hand, his laughter during a lucid spell, the comfort of routine. These memories, though tinged with sorrow, are treasures I carry. I share this in hopes that others might find comfort in knowing they’re not alone, and that even amid Alzheimer’s challenges, love and grace endure.
