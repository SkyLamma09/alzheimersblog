Lifestyle Prevention Factors

Growing evidence suggests lifestyle modifications may reduce Alzheimer's risk or slow progression:

1. The MIND Diet: Combining Mediterranean and DASH diets, this eating pattern emphasizes berries, leafy greens, nuts, and whole grains. Studies show it may reduce Alzheimer's risk by up to 53% for those following it closely.

2. Exercise Benefits: Regular aerobic exercise increases brain volume and improves connectivity. Just 150 minutes per week of moderate activity can reduce risk by 45%. Even simple walking shows protective effects.

3. Sleep & Stress: Chronic poor sleep increases amyloid accumulation, while stress management techniques like meditation may protect brain health. The RECONNECT trial showed mindfulness training improved cognitive scores in at-risk seniors.
